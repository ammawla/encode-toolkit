# Scientific Writing — Literature References

**Last updated:** 2026-03-07
**Purpose:** Reference catalog for the scientific-writing skill — papers on scientific communication standards, methods reproducibility guidelines, and the key publications defining best practices for reporting genomics experiments.

## Reporting Standards

---

### ENCODE Project Consortium 2020 — Expanded encyclopaedias of DNA elements

- **Citation:** ENCODE Project Consortium et al. Expanded encyclopaedias of DNA elements in the human and mouse genomes. Nature, 583(7818):699-710, 2020.
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41586-020-2493-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2493-4)
- **PMID:** 32728249 | **PMC:** PMC7410828
- **Citations:** ~2,500
- **Key findings:** ENCODE Phase 3 paper serving as the primary model for how large-scale functional genomics data should be reported. Exemplifies the standard for data availability statements (all data deposited in the ENCODE portal with accession numbers), methods descriptions (uniform processing pipeline specifications with version numbers), and supplementary material organization (metadata tables, QC metrics, processing parameters). This paper's reporting format is the template for methods sections generated by this skill.

---

### Landt et al. 2012 — ChIP-seq guidelines and practices

- **Citation:** Landt SG, Marinov GK, Kundaje A, et al. ChIP-seq guidelines and practices of the ENCODE and modENCODE consortia. Genome Research, 22(9):1813-1831, 2012.
- **DOI:** [10.1101/gr.136184.111](https://doi.org/10.1101/gr.136184.111)
- **PMID:** 22955991 | **PMC:** PMC3431496
- **Citations:** ~2,200
- **Key findings:** Established the reporting standard for ChIP-seq experiments that this skill extends to all ENCODE assays. Required reporting elements: antibody catalog number and lot, library preparation protocol, sequencing platform and read length, alignment tool and version, peak caller and parameters, QC metrics (FRiP, NSC, RSC, NRF), number of peaks called, and IDR reproducibility analysis. This paper is cited whenever ChIP-seq methods sections are generated.

---

### Conesa et al. 2016 — Best practices for RNA-seq data analysis

- **Citation:** Conesa A, Madrigal P, Tarazona S, et al. A survey of best practices for RNA-seq data analysis. Genome Biology, 17:13, 2016.
- **DOI:** [10.1186/s13059-016-0881-8](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-016-0881-8)
- **PMID:** 26813401 | **PMC:** PMC4728800
- **Citations:** ~4,500
- **Key findings:** Comprehensive RNA-seq best-practices review that defines the standard for RNA-seq methods reporting: experimental design (number of replicates, randomization), quality control metrics (mapping rate, rRNA contamination, replicate correlation), quantification method (counts, TPM, FPKM with explanation of normalization), and statistical testing (tool, correction method). This paper is cited when generating RNA-seq methods sections.

## Methods Reproducibility

---

### Sandve et al. 2013 — Ten simple rules for reproducible computational research

- **Citation:** Sandve GK, Nekrutenko A, Taylor J, Hovig E. Ten simple rules for reproducible computational research. PLoS Computational Biology, 9(10):e1003285, 2013.
- **DOI:** [10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003285](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003285)
- **PMID:** 24204232 | **PMC:** PMC3812051
- **Citations:** ~2,000
- **Key findings:** Established the foundational principles for reproducible computational research that inform this skill's methods generation: (1) track every step — log all commands and parameters, (2) avoid manual data manipulation, (3) archive exact versions of all software, (4) version-control all custom scripts, (5) record all intermediate results, (6) note random seeds and randomization order, (7) store raw data behind plots, (8) generate hierarchical analysis output, (9) connect textual statements to underlying results, (10) provide public access to scripts, runs, and results. The provenance logging in the data-provenance skill feeds directly into methods generation by this skill.

---

### Wilkinson et al. 2016 — FAIR data principles

- **Citation:** Wilkinson MD, Dumontier M, Aalbersberg IJ, et al. The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship. Scientific Data, 3:160018, 2016.
- **DOI:** [10.1038/sdata.2016.18](https://doi.org/10.1038/sdata.2016.18)
- **PMID:** 26978244 | **PMC:** PMC4792175
- **Citations:** ~8,000
- **Key findings:** Established the FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) for scientific data management. Data availability statements generated by this skill follow FAIR principles by providing ENCODE accession numbers (Findable), portal URLs (Accessible), standard file formats like bedMethyl and narrowPeak (Interoperable), and complete metadata including processing parameters (Reusable). ENCODE data is inherently FAIR-compliant through the portal infrastructure.

## Figure and Table Standards

---

### Rougier et al. 2014 — Ten simple rules for better figures

- **Citation:** Rougier NP, Droettboom M, Bing PE. Ten simple rules for better figures. PLoS Computational Biology, 10(9):e1003833, 2014.
- **DOI:** [10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003833](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003833)
- **PMID:** 25210732 | **PMC:** PMC4161295
- **Citations:** ~600
- **Key findings:** Guidelines for scientific figure design that inform the figure legend templates in this skill. Key principles: know your audience, identify the message, adapt the figure to the medium, use color effectively, avoid "chartjunk," and ensure the figure can stand alone with its legend. Figure legends generated by this skill follow the structure: panel description, data source (accession numbers), processing details, and statistical annotations.

## Citation Standards

---

### ENCODE citation guidelines

- **URL:** [https://www.encodeproject.org/help/citing-encode/](https://www.encodeproject.org/help/citing-encode/)
- **Key findings:** ENCODE requires citation of both the consortium paper (ENCODE Project Consortium 2020) and individual experiment accessions in any publication using ENCODE data. Data availability statements must include experiment accessions (ENCSR*), file accessions (ENCFF*), and the portal URL. This skill auto-generates these citations from provenance logs, ensuring all required accessions are included in the methods and data availability sections.

## Supplementary Table Standards

The scientific-writing skill generates four standard supplementary tables following the conventions established in ENCODE consortium publications:

- **Table S1** (Experiment Metadata): Accessions, biosample, assay, library strategy, platform, read length, paired/single, lab, date
- **Table S2** (QC Metrics): Assay-specific quality metrics per sample (FRiP, NSC, RSC for ChIP-seq; TSS enrichment for ATAC-seq; mapping rate, rRNA rate for RNA-seq; conversion rate for WGBS)
- **Table S3** (Differential Results): Statistical results with gene/peak ID, fold change, p-value, adjusted p-value, tool and version
- **Table S4** (Peak/Region Summary): Genomic coordinates, annotation, signal values, supporting evidence

These formats are derived from supplementary tables in ENCODE consortium publications and Dr. Mawla's published methods (Mawla & Huising 2019, Cell Metabolism; Mawla et al. 2023, Cell Reports).
